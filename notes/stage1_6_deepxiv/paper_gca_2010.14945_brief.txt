  "tldr": "[research paper] The paper proposes Graph Contrastive Learning with Adaptive Augmentation (GCA), a novel unsupervised framework that introduces adaptive data augmentation schemes tailored to graph topology and node attributes. Unlike uniform augmentation methods (e.g., random edge dropping or feature shuffling), GCA uses centrality-based priors to selectively corrupt unimportant edges and features—removing edges with low centrality and adding noise to low-impact feature dimensions—thereby preserving intrinsic graph structures and forcing the model to learn robust, semantically meaningful representations. Extensive experiments on real-world datasets show GCA outperforms state-of-the-art contrastive and supervised baselines in node classification, validating the effectiveness of adaptive augmentation in improving representation quality and invariance to perturbations.",
