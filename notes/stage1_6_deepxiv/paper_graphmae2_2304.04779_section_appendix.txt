Theorem for Local Clustering.\n\n###### Theorem A.1 (Theorem 1 in (Zhu et al., [2013](#bib.bib60 \"\")); Theorem 4.3 in (Yin et al., [2017](#bib.bib51 \"\"))).\n\nLet $T\\subset V$ be some unknown targeted cluster\nthat we are trying to retrieve from an unweighted graph.\nLet $\\eta$ be the inverse mixing time of the random walk on the subgraph\ninduced by $T$.\nThen there exists $T^{g}\\subseteq T$\nwith $\\operatorname{vol}{(T^{g})}\\geq\\operatorname{vol}{(T)}/2$, such that\nfor any seed $u\\in T^{g}$,\nPPR-Nibble with $\\alpha\\=\\Theta(\\eta)$ and\n$\\epsilon\\in\\left[\\frac{1}{10\\operatorname{vol}{(T)}},\\frac{1}{5\\operatorname{vol}{(T)}}\\right]$ outputs a set $S$ with\n\n|  | $\\Phi(S)\\leq\\widetilde{O}\\left(\\min\\left{\\sqrt{\\Phi(T)},\\Phi(T)/\\sqrt{\\eta}\\right}\\right).$ |  |\n| --- | --- | --- |\n\nHere, $\\operatorname{vol}{(S)}\\triangleq\\sum_{v_{i}\\in\\mathcal{S}}degree(v_{i})$ is the graph volume, and $\\Phi(S)\\triangleq\\frac{\\sum_{v_{i}\\in\\mathcal{S}}\\sum_{v_{j}\\in\\mathcal{V}-\\mathcal{S}}{\\bm{A}}(i,j)}{\\min(\\operatorname{vol}{(\\mathcal{S})},\\operatorname{vol}{(\\mathcal{V}-\\mathcal{S})})}$ defines the conductance of a non-empty node set $\\mathcal{S}\\subset\\mathcal{V}$.\n\n### A.2.
